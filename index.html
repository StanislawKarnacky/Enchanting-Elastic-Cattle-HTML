How to use stable diffusion

About Features Pricing Team Blog
You should download and install all dependeces. All info about it you can find here . After you
done i recommend you to test your skills as it is. It's pretty fun to input some text and see how
stable diffusion respond to it. As you can see we have several options in the top of screen:

                    txt2img will take our text and will turn it into image.
                     img2img will take image and try to remake it with our text description.
                    Extras is resize option only. But works very well. I use it a lot.

      1) txt2img . First of all you should clearly understand what do you want to see. 
For example i imagine a girl in the autumn forest , holding umbrella . It's rainy and chilly.  So its logical to input girl, autumn forest, holding umbrella, rainy. But if you do so, the result  will be far away from what you see on the internet today.  The reason of it - style. In fact we don't want just "girl in the forest". We want intricate, oil painted girl, sexy, with big breast.  You literally should describe what do you want to see to get the result you expect. The style, clothes, lighiting.  Every aspect of picture that you imagine. Big help is to use some of artist style by using name,  like Michelangelo, instead of detail description. Because just one name include a lot of aspects. You can combine names to get result that you want. In this example i added 3 artist and some words to make description more precise: 

Start with: Forest, rain, autumn, girl, femmine, big breast, long legs, umbrella, oil painting, Edmund Leighton, John William Waterhouse, Michelangelo

Now we got pretty good description, but what about finalisation? We need to take step back and see a bigger picture. We want to see a girl from distance
in full growth, from behind. It's rainy so we can add dark lighting. We want it to be detailed and sharp. It's important that neural network focus more on words that come at the beginning . The farther the  words from begining - the lighter the effect. And we can group the words by meaning using dots . Sometimes it's critical, sometimes not. Mess around and see by yourself. And finaly we can make words stronger by adding brackets and weaker by adding square brackets. The more brackets - the more unfluence word gets:

Final: european girl, femmine. ((perfect face)), ((detailed eyes)).(artist name), (artist name), artist name, ((artist name)). oil painting.((big breast)), (wide hips), (perfect legs). graceful pose. stockings, (victorian clothes). umbrella. forest, atmospheric, rainy. full growth, (detailed), sharp.

tip: i don't include artist names, but i won't leave you with empty hands. You can try your luck here and here .

Negative field is needed to prevent neural network from failures. We don't want to see mutants with extra legs, arms, mutated, more than two head and etc. Input here everything that you don't want to see.

Negative:   (((extra arms))), (((extra legs))), ((mutated)), ((more than two heads)), ((ugly)),  ((mutilated)), gross proportions,(((mutation))), extra fingers, ((extra limbs)), (malformed limbs), mutated hands, ((missing arms)), ((missing legs)),((badly drawn hands)), ((poorly drawn face)), ((mutated eyes)), ((ugly eyes)), (((deformed))), ((bad anatomy)), (((bad proportions))), cloned face, (((duplicate))), ((morbid)), (((disfigured))), (((more than 2 nipples))), extra limbs, (fused fingers), (bad anatomy), mutated hands, (too many fingers), (((long neck))), ((furniture with bad proportions)), ((wrong proportions)), out of frame, [[[adult]]], blurry

Settings:  
Sampling steps - i prefer between 30 and 50 . The larger the numbers - the longer the wait.
Sampling method : i use DIMM , but sometimes play around with Eulers .
Witdth/height - the larger the numbers - the longer the wait. 512*704 is pretty good for start.
Restore faces - be cure to check this box.
Batch count - how much batches do you want. For tests i recommend to use 1 . It let you to see results pretty fast in browser(but also you can see it in output directory). As soon as your text do what you want it to do you can go all in to value 16 .
Batch size . If your graphic card has less than 6GB , use lower values (1 or 2). For everyone else i recommend start with 3 and go higher (if there is no errors)
CFG scale . As i get it's a finalization value. Sharpness and contrast together. Between 7 and 12 it gets me what i want. But you free to play around to find the best for yourself.

      2)  img2img - the same rules, as in previous. It tries to make img based on your sourse image. Works pretty bad with faces. For now can't find a way to save even facial features. It makes new faces every time. But it saves part of clothes, pose. Pretty fun to mess with.

      3) Extras . Obviously i use it to uspcale images. Settings: 4 times upscale, upsclaer one is Lancoz , upscaler two is ESRGAN 4x . In a bunch it gives pretty detailed, sharp image. But not too oversharp, like double ESRGAN.
